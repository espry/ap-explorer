About the Avoidable Poverty Index

Interactive companion to How Much Poverty Could Redistribution End? (Prydz, Lind, Moene). Every number here is recomputed in your browser from each economy's World Bank PIP distribution (100 equal-population percentiles, daily household welfare in 2021 PPP), so you can change the poverty line, the affluence line and the mobilization rate and watch the avoidable–structural decomposition respond. PIP data only; 172 economies, latest survey wave with a 1990–2024 series.

The lines

Poverty line zp
Default is the floorless Societal Poverty Line, zp = 1.30 + 0.5 × median. Switch to relative-to-mean, a fixed $/day line, or add a floor.
Affluence line zr
Default 4 × zp (≈ twice the median). Can also be set as a multiple of the median, a top percentile, or a fixed $/day threshold.

Accounting (no calibration)

G — poverty gap
Mean shortfall below zp, per capita ($/day).
S — affluent surplus
Mean income above zr, per capita ($/day).
τ* — required rate
τ* = G/S: the share of the affluent surplus that would close the poverty gap at perfect transfer efficiency. τ* < 1 means the surplus exceeds the gap.
Capacity
S/G = 1/τ*.

The decomposition (uses f and β)

f — mobilization rate
Fraction of the affluent surplus a state can actually mobilize (f = e·τ, the surtax times its pass-through). Mobilizable resources M = f·S. Baseline f = 0.40.
PA — avoidable poverty
min{G, M}: the gap closable by domestic redistribution.
PS — structural poverty
max{0, G − M}: the residual genuine scarcity. G ≡ PA + PS.
ξ / AGR — avoidable share
ξ = min{1, M/G} = min{1, f/τ*}. The share of the gap that is avoidable.
APβ — Avoidable Poverty Index
PA + βPS, counting avoidable poverty in full and structural poverty at a discount β ∈ [0,1). Shown normalized by zp for cross-country comparability (= [ξ + β(1−ξ)] × FGT gap index). β = 0.50 baseline.

At the baseline (zp floorless SPL, zr = 4zp, f = 0.40, β = 0.50) these reproduce the paper's headline figures. Formulas: paper §2.
